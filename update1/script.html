HOW TO WRITE IN BAYBAYIN

Baybayin is an alpha-syllabic script used by the Tagalogs during the pre-colonial period. An alphasyllabary means that consonants are partnered with vowels to make a syllable sound. The script consists of 3 alphabet characters for the vowels (A, E/I, and O/U) and 14 alphabet characters representing syllabic consonants (B, C/K, D/R, G, H, L, M, N, NG, P, S, T, W, and Y).

VOWELS

The vowels in Baybayin is one of the interesting things to note when learning this system of writing. Baybayin only has three vowels as opposed to the conventional five — A, E/I, and O/U. Both E/I and O/U share the same character.

DID YOU KNOW...

Banana leaves used to be one of the main materials that the early Tagalogs wrote on. This explains why there are few accounts present about Baybayin scripts.

RULES

Now that you have memorized the consonants partnered by the “A” vowel, it is time to know how to change the “A” vowel into “E/I” or “O/U” and how to cancel the vowel sound.

To replace the vowel “A” with “E/I,” you just have to put a dot above the character. On the other hand, putting a dot below the character will replace any consonants’ vowel partnered sound into “O/U. Placing a plus sign below the character will remove any vowels in the syllable. Lastly, when writing in Baybayin, one must keep in mind that words should be spelt the way you say it. Take Perlas ng Silanganan for example.

DID YOU KNOW...

Baybayin was said to be a form of early Kawi Script which connects it to the Kingdom of Java. This hints at a historical connection between our descendants and the Indonesians.
